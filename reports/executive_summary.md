# Attrix — Executive Workforce Intelligence Summary

**Date:** July 15, 2026  
**Audience:** HR Leadership, Department Heads, Executive Board  
**Subject:** Diagnostic Intelligence on Voluntary Employee Departures at Palo Alto Networks

---

## 1. Executive Snapshot
An analysis of the **1,470 employee records** within the workforce dataset reveals that the organization’s overall voluntary attrition rate stands at **16.12%**, representing **237 employee exits** and **1,233 retained employees**. 

While this rate serves as the organizational baseline, attrition is not distributed evenly. It is concentrated heavily within specific functional departments, job roles, workload brackets, and early-tenure cohorts. Isolating these risk hotspots allows for targeted talent interventions.

---

## 2. Key Findings & Diagnostic Hotspots

### A. Functional Concentration (Sales & R&D)
* **Sales Department:** Has the highest observed department attrition rate at **20.63%** (representing 92 exits out of 446 employees). This is **4.5 percentage points above the organizational baseline**.
* **Research & Development (R&D):** Maintains a lower attrition rate of **13.84%**, but due to its large size (961 employees), it accounts for the largest absolute volume of exits: **133 departures (56.1% of all organizational exits)**.
* **Critical Roles:** **Sales Representatives** experience an extremely high attrition rate of **39.76%** (33 exits out of 83 roles). **Laboratory Technicians** in R&D also exhibit elevated attrition at **23.94%** (62 exits), representing the highest volume for any single role.

### B. Workload Demands (Overtime & Travel)
* **Overtime Burnout:** Employees working overtime show an attrition rate of **30.50%** (127 exits), compared to only **10.42%** (110 exits) for employees who do not work overtime. Overtime is associated with a **3x increase** in observed attrition rate.
* **Travel Demands:** Frequent business travelers experience an attrition rate of **24.91%** (69 exits), compared to **14.96%** (156 exits) for rare travelers.
* **The High-Stress Matrix:** Employees logging both **Overtime** and **Frequent Business Travel** have an observed attrition rate of **52.38%**—a Workload Attrition Index of **3.25x** relative to the organizational baseline.

### C. Career Stage & Onboarding Friction
* **Early-Tenure Volatility:** Employees within their first **2 years** of employment account for **125 exits (52.7% of all departures)**. The attrition rate for employees in their first year is **34.7%** (29 exits out of 83).
* **Manager Transition:** Employees reporting to a new manager (under 1 year of alignment) show an attrition rate of **32.2%** (38 exits), suggesting friction during leadership transitions.

---

## 3. Prioritized Strategic Interventions

### Priority 1: Early-Tenure Integration Pilot
* **Target:** All new hires (tenure ≤ 2 years), focusing on Sales Representatives and Lab Technicians.
* **Action:** Institutionalize structured onboarding checkpoints at 30, 60, and 90 days. Implement a peer-mentorship program to ease cultural and team transitions.
* **Success Measure:** First-year retention improvement of 15% within 12 months.

### Priority 2: Overtime Capacity & Governance
* **Target:** High-stress groups logging over 10 hours of overtime per week.
* **Action:** Require manager authorization for prolonged overtime. Perform resource audits to identify hiring gaps in teams where overtime is chronic.
* **Success Measure:** Average monthly overtime hours reduced by 25%.

### Priority 3: Travel Policy Adjustments
* **Target:** Frequent Business Travelers.
* **Action:** Audit travel requirements and implement a virtual-first meeting guideline. Ensure travelers receive flexible work-from-home days immediately following multi-day travel.
* **Success Measure:** Travel satisfaction improvement, decrease in travel-related exits.
